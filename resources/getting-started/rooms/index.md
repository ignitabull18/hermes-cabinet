# Rooms

Rooms are switchable workspaces inside Cabinet, and here is the whole idea in one picture: **Cabinet is your home, each room is a space inside it (Office, Study, Research, and so on), and every room has one big cabinet where all of that room's things are kept.** Switching rooms is like walking into a different room of the house: a different cabinet, with a different set of files, a different team of agents, a different task board, and even a different look.

## Home and rooms

Each room has its own **big cabinet**, and that one cabinet holds everything in the room:

- **Files**: the room's pages and folders.
- **Team**: the agents that belong to the room.
- **Tasks and jobs**: the room's task board and scheduled runs.
- **Skills**: the skills installed into the room.
- **Look**: the room's own theme, icon, and color.

Nothing in one room's cabinet leaks into another, and two rooms can even use different themes at the same time.

On disk it stays simple: your home is the data folder, and each room is a folder inside it (that folder is the room's cabinet). The cabinet you started in is your default room.

## The room switcher

The current room's icon sits next to the **cabinet** logo at the top of the sidebar. Click it to open the switcher. The room name shows just below (in the drawer header and the main view), so the top stays compact and the icon is all you need up there.

From the switcher you can:

- **Switch rooms**: click any room in the list. The sidebar, Team, Tasks, and theme all update to that room.
- **Open in new window**: open a room in its own window. Each window keeps its own room, so you can have the Office open in one window and Research in another, side by side. This works in both the desktop app and the browser.
- **Customize this room**: set the room's name, icon, color, and theme.
- **Add room**: create a new room.

## Customizing a room

Open the switcher and choose **Customize this room**. You can set:

- **Name**: the display name shown in the switcher and headers.
- **Icon**: pick one from the icon set, or leave it blank to use the room's initial letter.
- **Color**: pick an accent color, or leave it on **Auto** to get a stable color derived from the room. Each room shows as a small colored tile (its icon or initial in its color), so rooms are easy to tell apart at a glance.
- **Theme**: pick a theme just for this room, or choose **Use global default** to follow your app-wide theme. The theme applies the moment you switch into the room.

## The default (home) room

Your original cabinet is the default room. It behaves like any other room: its sidebar shows only its own pages, not the other rooms. You reach the other rooms through the switcher, not by browsing into them. That keeps each room focused on its own contents.

## Adding a room

Choose **Add room** in the switcher to create a new room (a new top-level cabinet). Give it a name and, if you like, pick a few agents to start with. Once created, it appears in the switcher, ready to customize with its own icon, color, and theme.

> **Tip.** A room's cabinet works like any other cabinet in the app, so everything you already know (pages, agents, tasks, skills, knowledge links) works exactly the same inside a room.
